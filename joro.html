About Work

Joro
UI Design and Information Architecture

Project

I was asked to help Joro with the creation of their iPhone app by designing their information and user interface architecture. I worked on making high fidelity mockups of their Purchase Carbon Offset userflow, and created their Give a Gift userflow.

Problem

Joro had rough sketches and mockups of their carbon offset flow but needed me to adjust the visual hierarchy and produce high fidelity mockups for their engineering team. I then needed to apply this styling and IA structure to an undeveloped give a gift flow.

Role

I assumed the role of a freelance product designer for this project, meeting with Joro over video conference and discussing the project via email.

Final user interface architecture for the carbon offset flow

Final user interface architecture for the give a gift flow

Solution

Joro's mission is to help reduce carbon emissions by making it easy for users to purchase carbon offsets. I felt it was important to make the carbon offset and give a gift user flows sleek, simple, and informative for the user.

The final user interface relies heavliy on Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. Sticking to the HIG relativly tightly gave the app a very fast and native feel and made it easier for the Joro engineers to quickly implement.

Prior User Research

Before I joined this project, Joro had conducted preliminary user research to get an idea of what potential users already knew about carbon emissions, and to test some low fidelity user flows.

The users showed interest in knowing how they were helping the envirnoment through Joro and as always, exactly how much they were paying and for what.

Joro's original early mockups

Early Work

I began this restructuring by digging into what work Joro already had and thinking about how to make the user flows clear, educational, and intuitive. I marked up the screens, and sketched out a few new ideas to present to Joro.

A selection of initial logo sketches.

High Fidelity Iterations

I took these mockups, notes, and ideas to Joro to discuss what they felt were the most important pieces of information to highlight based on user research. After picking their brains, I dove into creating a system of information hierarchy through font styles and UI work focusing on clarity of information.

A selection of higher fidelity mockups.

Finishing Touches and Type

After presenting my higher fidelity mockups to Slapdash, there were some designs that stood out to them that I further worked on to make sure we were happy with every detail. At this point I started testing out different typefaces and looking at how they interacted with the logo. With hundreds of iterations in the books, I arrived at the final logo and wordmark that Slapdash was proud to launch version 1.0 with.
